Summary

Policy Making with AI is a decision support platform that harnesses the power of domain models using AI in a trusted environment, bringing together policy- and decision- makers, modelers, and AI practitioners to support complex decision-making.

Decision-makers must plan and implement interventions and strategies at varying timescales across different populations and geographies. This could be to control the spread of a pandemic like COVID-19, or to manage climate impact risks on global supply chains. Models are essential in the planning process but are often complex, uncertain, and can be difficult to consume, and also need to be trusted. The space of possible interventions that the models represent is vast, making the planning and decision-making process even more difficult.

Policymaking with AI (PMAI) contains a core AI decisioning library using algorithms from the sub-disciplines of Planning, Reinforcement Learning and Optimization to enable uncertainty quantification, impact analysis, and exploration of the full space of possible options to inform decision making. Further, PMAI is built on top of Hyperledger fabric to support model and data governance and provide trust and transparency for understanding the provenance of data, models, and results generated by end-users

PMAI provides decision-makers with easy navigation through complex intervention spaces in a timely manner is required to generate the most optimal and context-relevant intervention programs, including: 1.) An interactive decision dashboard to fine-tune what is important for their context (including what-if analysis, and adding constraints). 2.) The ability to combine, learn, and compare multiple models. 3.) Calibration and uncertainty quantification which are essential to decision-making based on the real-world observational data, as misleading information could be catastrophic for high stakes decisions (e.g. healthcare).

Specifically, there are three ways in which what if analysis is performed using PMAI: 1.) What if analysis where a decision maker defines intervention packages of interest and the platform runs them and generates predictions. 2.) What if analysis where a decision maker defines a desired outcome trajectory and the platform uses AI algorithms to find intervention packages that meet the desired outcome trajectory. 3.) Algorithm controlled what-if analysis within the constraints set by a modeler with the goal of interrogating the intervention space to reach at candidate intervention packages that a decision maker can filter through depending on their needs. Our algorithms are developed to learn in the presence of uncertainty and sample the huge intervention space while optimizing the user defined goal. In the real world there are many sources of uncertainty, accordingly, to generate recommendations which are robust the algorithms must be able to learn in the presence of uncertainty.

With trust at its core, this decision support platform is designed to amplify the value from domain models and data, and it is a demonstration of the value which can be derived from AI and Hybrid Cloud technologies.
